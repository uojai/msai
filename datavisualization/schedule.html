Chart Types and Design

In this session, students will transition from simply "making graphs" to mastering the art of visual storytelling through the 4-S Rule (Simple, Small, Scalable, and Storytelling). We will dive deep into the specific use cases for core chart types—including bar charts for comparison, line charts for trends, and heatmaps for density—learning not just how to build them in Matplotlib, but when each serves as the most effective lens for your data. By applying professional design principles like high-contrast "dark mode" styling, strategic color use, and intentional labeling, students will gain the practical skills needed to transform raw livestock and agricultural datasets into clear, executive-level insights that drive real-world decision-making.

Week 3

Jan 30, 2026

Data Exploration and Preparation

In this interactive lecture, students will master the essential bridge between raw data collection and impactful storytelling. We explore the critical "Data Preparation" phase, teaching students how to move beyond messy spreadsheets by standardizing entries, rescuing missing values through imputation, and filtering out technical noise. Through a series of graphical "lenses"—ranging from histograms for distribution analysis to heatmaps for density patterns—students learn how to select the right visualization for identifying correlations and long-term trends. By the end of the session, learners will have the practical skills to transform chaotic livestock sensor logs into clean, executive-level summaries that drive real-world decision-making.

Week 2

Jan 23, 2026

The Anatomy of a Chart

This lecture, "The Anatomy of a Chart," serves as the foundational bridge between the psychological "why" of human vision and the technical "how" of data visualization design. We move beyond the automatic grouping rules of Gestalt psychology to explore the "Grammar of Graphics," a systematic framework for mapping abstract data onto physical marks and visual channels. By deconstructing charts into their core building blocks—such as position, color, and size—students will learn to navigate the hierarchy of perceptual accuracy to ensure their designs are both truthful and effective. Through a practical case study involving a community livestock dashboard, we demonstrate how to select the most potent visual channels to transform raw numbers into intuitive, actionable insights for any audience.

Marks & Channels: The Atoms of Visualization
Mapping Magnitude: Visualizing "How Much"
Mapping Identity: Visualizing "What Kind"

  • Secondary Reading: Chapter 2 (What: Data Abstraction)

Week 1

Jan 16, 2026

Introduction and Logistics

This lecture provides a comprehensive introduction to the art and science of data visualization, beginning with an exploration of why translating raw numbers into visual formats is essential for efficient decision-making and storytelling. By examining how the human brain processes and interprets visual information—leveraging concepts like pre-attentive attributes and cognitive load—students will gain a deeper understanding of the biological basis for effective charts. The session concludes with a practical deep dive into core design principles, such as establishing a clear visual hierarchy, utilizing high-contrast elements to direct attention, and applying strategic color theory to ensure clarity and accessibility in every graphic.
